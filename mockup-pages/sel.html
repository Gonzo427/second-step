Second Step SEL

  • Fun to learn
  • Easy to teach
  • Research-based

Supported by music and videos, take-home activities, and stories kids relate to, the developmentally appropriate Second Step lessons have helped teachers instill social-emotional skills in their students for over 20 years. Best of all, the student lessons are easy to teach, right out of the box!

Trusted By Schools

School leaders choose the Second Step program because it's trusted by 26,000+ schools worldwide. It aligns with district standards, and SEL improves student achievement.

Engaging And Useful

Students like the Second Step program because the lessons are engaging and the skills taught are useful in school, on the playground, and at home.

Easy To Use

Educators give the Second Step program a thumbs-up because it's easy to use and can improve classroom behavior, which improves learning.

Endorsed

The Second Step program has been endorsed by numerous organizations, including the Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning, for the quality of its research, training and teacher and family support. Learn more about how Second Step aligns with academic standards.

Solutions By Grade

Early Learning

Your youngest learners will benefit more from preschool and be better prepared for kindergarten with self-regulation and executive-functions skills that help them pay attention, remember directions, and control their behavior.

Learn More

Elementary

Students in grades K–5 continue developing their social-emotional skills—including making friends, managing emotions, and solving problems—to set them on the path for social success and academic readiness.

Learn More

Middle School

Middle-schoolers are better prepared to navigate adolescence with communication, coping, and decision-making skills that help them make good choices and avoid pitfalls, such as peer pressure, substance abuse, and bullying.

Learn More
